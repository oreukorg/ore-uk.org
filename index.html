What you actually do

No stock photos or demo videos needed: every format mirrors how you work in the exam cubicle or at the desk. You type, choose, and converse — the platform scores your real inputs.

304 scenarios

Actor OSCE stations

Read the station brief, then chat with a simulated patient or relative under exam timing.

  • One-minute reading time, then five-minute active station (official-style timing)
  • Type your questions and explanations — the patient responds in character
  • Hidden clinical facts unlock only when you ask appropriately (like a real actor station)
  • Emotion and trust shift based on how you communicate
15 tasks

Written desk stations

Unstaffed desk work: prescriptions, radiograph reports, HTM audit forms, treatment plans, and referral letters.

  • Fill structured forms and free-text fields — not multiple choice
  • Radiograph stations score against verification tables for findings you report
  • Prescription and HTM tasks check dose, safety, and documentation detail
  • Submit when ready; marking runs against the station rubric
8 topics

Practical study topics

Self-practice checklists for hands-on technique — examiner mark-sheet style, not a live manikin exam.

  • Work through step-by-step procedures at your own pace
  • Tick off checklist items as you would on an examiner sheet
  • Band your overall performance (exceeds / meets / below standard)
  • See which critical steps you missed before you sit the real OSCE
11 themes · 180 questions

Medical emergencies

Oral viva stems plus verbal BLS practice — procedural timing and examiner questions, not hands-on CPR coaching.

  • Answer viva questions aloud in your own words (typed submission)
  • Verbal BLS walkthrough with timing prompts aligned to examiner format
  • Themes cover anaphylaxis, asthma, hypoglycaemia, syncope, arrest, emergency drugs, and more
  • Structured review against canonical management steps
24 slots

17-station circuit mock

Run a continuous mock exam session — actor, written, and practical slots sequenced with rest and pilot breaks that do not score.

  • Official-style circuit flow: active stations, rest slots, and pilot slots
  • Station-to-station timing like exam day
  • Build stamina and pacing before you pay for a physical mock course
  • Review each station after the circuit or return to weak areas in the library

How it works

From registration to review in four steps — the same flow for OSCE stations, circuit mocks, and medical emergencies.

1

Choose your focus

Start with the free trial OSCE station and ME theme, or browse the full library after you subscribe.

2

Run under exam conditions

Reading time, timers, and station rules match official OSCE / ME format. Interact with the patient, complete forms, or answer viva stems.

3

Submit and finish

End the station when time is up or you have completed the task. Your inputs are saved for marking.

4

Review and improve

Open your feedback report: domains, checklists, critical fails, and guidance. Reattempt on paid Pro plans while you still have allowance.

Feedback based on your performance

When you end a session, review is generated from what you submitted — transcript, forms, checklist ticks, or viva answers — not generic tips.

Pass / fail and domain scores

Overall outcome plus rubric domains (history, diagnosis, management, communication, professionalism) scored against station criteria.

Checklist guidance

Item-by-item breakdown of what you met, missed, or critically failed — linked to GDC-style marking language.

Strengths and gaps

Concrete strengths, improvements, and missed items drawn from your session, with evidence quotes from your transcript where relevant.

Common mistake callouts

Pitfalls catalogued for that station type — surfaced when your attempt matches a known weak pattern.

Official guidance links

ORE-aligned guidance excerpts and learning resources so you know what examiners expect next time.
